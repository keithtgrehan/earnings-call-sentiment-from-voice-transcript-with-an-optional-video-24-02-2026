# Product Scoping & Roadmap

## Product Goals
- Reduce time needed to produce a first-pass earnings-call signal summary versus manual transcript review in capstone user tests.
- Improve consistency of call interpretation by using a repeatable, transcript-first signal workflow.
- Ensure each key signal in the summary is linked to transcript evidence for auditable review.

## Key Opportunities
- Manual transcript review is flexible but too slow and noisy for timely retail decision support.
- Generic summaries are fast but often too broad for guidance-shift and tone-change interpretation.
- Institutional-style workflows are powerful but generally inaccessible for most retail users.
- A focused middle ground can deliver structured, evidence-backed signal review without heavy tooling.

## Core Initiatives
- Deliver structured earnings-call signal extraction using guidance shifts, tone-change moments, and transcript evidence.
- Strengthen current-versus-prior guidance comparison so change direction is clearer.
- Improve signal prioritization so users see the most important moments first.
- Package outputs into a concise, demo-ready summary suitable for capstone evaluation.

## Roadmap
### Now
- Ship the transcript-first capstone MVP for one-call analysis.
- Generate structured outputs for guidance shifts, tone-change moments, and evidence snippets.
- Keep scope focused on decision support for retail trader review.

### Next
- Improve prioritization logic so high-signal moments are surfaced more clearly.
- Tighten guidance comparison outputs for faster interpretation of change direction.
- Refine output clarity and consistency for smoother user testing and demos.

### Later
- Explore broader cross-call benchmarking and iterative signal-quality improvements.
- Evaluate multimodal audio signals (speaking-rate change, pause / hesitation detection, emphasis / pitch variability, and alignment with transcript guidance statements).

## Assumptions and Constraints
- Capstone timelines require a narrow, practical feature scope and lightweight validation.
- The MVP remains transcript-first, with audio-expansion ideas reserved for later stages.
- The product is a decision-support tool and does not execute trades autonomously.
- The current phase prioritizes clarity, consistency, and auditable evidence over broad platform coverage.
