# Problem Definition & Value Proposition

## Target User
The primary user is a self-directed retail trader or active private investor who follows earnings season closely and wants to make faster, better-informed judgments after each call. This user is serious about fundamental signals but does not have the time, team support, or institutional tooling to deeply process every long transcript.

## Core Problem
The user’s challenge is not lack of information; it is overload. Earnings calls are long, noisy, and unstructured, which makes it hard to quickly isolate the few moments that matter, especially subtle guidance shifts, changes in management confidence, and tone-change signals.

## Context and Impact
When this problem is not solved, review is slow and inconsistent, and important signal-bearing moments can be missed or interpreted differently from one call to the next. Retail traders may rely on broad summaries without clear transcript evidence, making it harder to understand what actually changed and why it matters now.

## Value Proposition
The Earnings Call Signal Extraction Engine converts an earnings call into a structured, auditable signal summary focused on guidance shifts, tone-change moments, and transcript evidence. It helps retail traders review calls faster and more consistently by highlighting what changed, why it matters, and where to look first in the source text.

## Expected User Outcomes
- Faster post-call review with clearer prioritization of high-signal moments.
- More consistent interpretation across different calls and reporting periods.
- Stronger evidence-backed conclusions tied directly to transcript snippets.
- Higher confidence in what to review first without replacing user judgment.

## Scope Reminder
This product is a focused decision-support tool for retail trader earnings-call review. It is transcript-first and designed to structure interpretation, not automate trades. It does not attempt to replicate a full institutional research platform or act as an autonomous trading engine.
